ShipStation: Carrier Billing & VAT Setup Guide

Third-Party Billing for Duties & Taxes

Not Available. ShipStation does not support billing duties and taxes to a separate third-party account independently from shipping charges. Their third-party billing feature charges both shipping and duties/taxes to the same third-party account — there is no way to split them.

ShipStation's "Bill Int'l Duties to Payor of Shipping Charges" setting ties duty/tax billing to whoever pays for shipping. There are no separate fields for a duties-only third-party billing account like in other platforms (e.g., ShipHero).

This is a known feature gap. Users have requested this capability in the ShipStation community, but it has not been implemented.

Adding VAT Tax IDs

ShipStation stores tax identifiers in International Settings and makes them available for all international shipments.

Adding Tax Identifiers

  1. Go to Account Settings > Shipping > International Settings
  2. Enter your tax identifier information:
    • Tax ID Type (VAT, EORI, IOSS, etc.)
    • Tax ID Number
  3. Click Add Tax Identifier
  4. Click Save Tax Identifier
  5. To add more, click + Add Another Tax Identifier and repeat
Note: Tax ID details cannot be modified once added. To make a change, you must delete the entry and add a new one.

Using Tax IDs on Shipments

Once added, Tax IDs are available to attach to international shipments in two ways:

  • Manually — Select the Tax ID when configuring a shipment's customs declarations
  • Via Automation Rules — Add a Tax Identifier action to your automation rules to apply automatically

Tax ID Types

Type When to Use
VAT Required for doing business in the UK and EU countries. You may need separate VAT numbers per country.
EORI Used by customs to monitor and track shipments into/out of the EU.
IOSS Import One Stop Shop — for EU and non-EU businesses to pay VAT on EU imports. Must register for an IOSS number. If you don't use IOSS, your customers will pay VAT upon delivery.

Notes

  • Always include your company Tax ID numbers (VAT, IOSS, etc.) on your Customs Declarations
  • If you need your IOSS number, contact your selling channels to request it
  • The appropriate Tax ID depends on: where goods ship from, the destination country, and what goods you sell
